Sir Thomas sent friendly advice and professions, Lady Bertram dispatched money and babylinen, and Norris wrote the letters. Such were its immediate effects, and within a twelvemonth a more important advantage to Price resulted from it. Norris was often observing to the others that she could not get her poor sister and her family out of her head, and that, much as they had all done for her, she seemed to be wanting to do more. and at length she could not but own it to be her wish that poor Price should be relieved from the charge and expense of one child entirely out of her great number.  What if they were among them to undertake the care of her eldest daughter, a girl now nine years old, of an age to require more attention than her poor mother could possibly give?
