It was a good spot for faultfinding. Mr. Crawford was soon followed by Miss Bertram and Rushworth. and when, after a little time, the others began to form into parties, these three were found in busy consultation on the terrace by Edmund, Miss Crawford, and Fanny, who seemed as naturally to unite, and who, after a short participation of their regrets and difficulties, left them and walked on. The remaining three, Rushworth, Norris, and Julia, were still far behind. for Julia, whose happy star no longer prevailed, was obliged to keep by the side of Rushworth, and restrain her impatient feet to that lady's slow pace, while her aunt, having fallen in with the housekeeper, who was come out to feed the pheasants, was lingering behind in gossip with her.
