Capital season'd Hunter.   Fanny, meanwhile, vexed with herself for not having been as motionless as she was speechless, and grieved to the heart to see Edmund's arrangements, was trying by everything in the power of her modest, gentle nature, to repulse Crawford, and avoid both his looks and inquiries. and he, unrepulsable, was persisting in both.   What did that shake of the head mean? What was it meant to express?
