CHAPTER XVIII  Everything was now in a regular train: theatre, actors, actresses, and dresses, were all getting forward. but though no other great impediments arose, Fanny found, before many days were past, that it was not all uninterrupted enjoyment to the party themselves, and that she had not to witness the continuance of such unanimity and delight as had been almost too much for her at first. Everybody began to have their vexation. Edmund had many. Entirely against his judgment, a scenepainter arrived from town, and was at work, much to the increase of the expenses, and, what was worse, of the eclat of their proceedings.
