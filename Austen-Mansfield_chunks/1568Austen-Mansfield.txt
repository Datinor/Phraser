Edmund would be forgiven for being a clergyman, it seemed, under certain conditions of wealth. and this, she suspected, was all the conquest of prejudice which he was so ready to congratulate himself upon. She had only learnt to think nothing of consequence but money. CHAPTER XLVI  As Fanny could not doubt that her answer was conveying a real disappointment, she was rather in expectation, from her knowledge of Miss Crawford's temper, of being urged again. and though no second letter arrived for the space of a week, she had still the same feeling when it did come.
