I ought to have sent you an account of your cousin's first party, but I was lazy, and now it is too long ago. suffice it, that everything was just as it ought to be, in a style that any of her connexions must have been gratified to witness, and that her own dress and manners did her the greatest credit. My friend, Fraser, is mad for such a house, and it would not make me miserable. I go to Lady Stornaway after Easter.
