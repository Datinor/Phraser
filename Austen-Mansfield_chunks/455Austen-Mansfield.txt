CHAPTER XIV  Fanny seemed nearer being right than Edmund had supposed. The business of finding a play that would suit everybody proved to be no trifle. and the carpenter had received his orders and taken his measurements, had suggested and removed at least two sets of difficulties, and having made the necessity of an enlargement of plan and expense fully evident, was already at work, while a play was still to seek. Other preparations were also in hand.
