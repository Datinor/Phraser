They were a remarkably fine family, the sons very welllooking, the daughters decidedly handsome, and all of them wellgrown and forward of their age, which produced as striking a difference between the cousins in person, as education had given to their address. and no one would have supposed the girls so nearly of an age as they really were. There were in fact but two years between the youngest and Fanny. Julia Bertram was only twelve, and Maria but a year older. The little visitor meanwhile was as unhappy as possible.
