Bertram is certainly well off for a cadet of even a baronet's family. By the time he is four or five and twenty he will have seven hundred a year, and nothing to do for it.   Miss Crawford could have said that there would be a something to do and to suffer for it, which she could not think lightly of. but she checked herself and let it pass. and tried to look calm and unconcerned when the two gentlemen shortly afterwards joined them.
