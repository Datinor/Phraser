He had received a note from Lady Stornaway to beg him to call. and regarding it as what was meant to be the last, last interview of friendship, and investing her with all the feelings of shame and wretchedness which Crawford's sister ought to have known, he had gone to her in such a state of mind, so softened, so devoted, as made it for a few moments impossible to Fanny's fears that it should be the last. But as he proceeded in his story, these fears were over. She had met him, he said, with a serious--certainly a serious--even an agitated air. but before he had been able to speak one intelligible sentence, she had introduced the subject in a manner which he owned had shocked him.
