for though she read in too much haste and confusion to form the clearest judgment of Miss Crawford's meaning, it was evident that she meant to compliment her on her brother's attachment, and even to appear to believe it serious. She did not know what to do, or what to think. There was wretchedness in the idea of its being serious. there was perplexity and agitation every way. She was distressed whenever Crawford spoke to her, and he spoke to her much too often.
