He cannot much longer deceive himself. I am sorry for them all, and would give something that Rushworth and Maria had never met.   In this quarter, indeed, disappointment was impending over Sir Thomas. Not all his goodwill for Rushworth, not all Rushworth's deference for him, could prevent him from soon discerning some part of the truth--that Rushworth was an inferior young man, as ignorant in business as in books, with opinions in general unfixed, and without seeming much aware of it himself. He had expected a very different soninlaw.
