And you must be aware, Fanny, that it is every young woman's duty to accept such a very unexceptionable offer as this.   This was almost the only rule of conduct, the only piece of advice, which Fanny had ever received from her aunt in the course of eight years and a half. It silenced her. She felt how unprofitable contention would be. If her aunt's feelings were against her, nothing could be hoped from attacking her understanding.
