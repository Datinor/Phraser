Time would undoubtedly abate somewhat of his sufferings, but still it was a sort of thing which he never could get entirely the better of. and as to his ever meeting with any other woman who could--it was too impossible to be named but with indignation. Fanny's friendship was all that he had to cling to. CHAPTER XLVIII  Let other pens dwell on guilt and misery.
