He had no idea of concealment. It was all known at the Parsonage, where he loved to talk over the future with both his sisters, and it would be rather gratifying to him to have enlightened witnesses of the progress of his success. When Sir Thomas understood this, he felt the necessity of making his own wife and sisterinlaw acquainted with the business without delay. though, on Fanny's account, he almost dreaded the effect of the communication to Norris as much as Fanny herself. He deprecated her mistaken but wellmeaning zeal.
