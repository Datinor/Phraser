She was small of her age, with no glow of complexion, nor any other striking beauty. exceedingly timid and shy, and shrinking from notice. but her air, though awkward, was not vulgar, her voice was sweet, and when she spoke her countenance was pretty. Sir Thomas and Lady Bertram received her very kindly. and Sir Thomas, seeing how much she needed encouragement, tried to be all that was conciliating: but he had to work against a most untoward gravity of deportment.
