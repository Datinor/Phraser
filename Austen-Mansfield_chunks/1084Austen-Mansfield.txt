Fanny thought she had never known a day of greater agitation, both of pain and pleasure. but happily the pleasure was not of a sort to die with the day. for every day would restore the knowledge of William's advancement, whereas the pain, she hoped, would return no more. She had no doubt that her note must appear excessively illwritten, that the language would disgrace a child, for her distress had allowed no arrangement.
