Their father was no object of love to them. he had never seemed the friend of their pleasures, and his absence was unhappily most welcome. They were relieved by it from all restraint. and without aiming at one gratification that would probably have been forbidden by Sir Thomas, they felt themselves immediately at their own disposal, and to have every indulgence within their reach. Fanny's relief, and her consciousness of it, were quite equal to her cousins'.
