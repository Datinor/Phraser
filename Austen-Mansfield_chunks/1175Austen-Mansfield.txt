It would not be fair to inquire into a young lady's exact estimate of her own perfections. In spite of his intended silence, Sir Thomas found himself once more obliged to mention the subject to his niece, to prepare her briefly for its being imparted to her aunts. a measure which he would still have avoided, if possible, but which became necessary from the totally opposite feelings of Crawford as to any secrecy of proceeding.
