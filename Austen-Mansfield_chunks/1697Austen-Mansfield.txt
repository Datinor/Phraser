He might have made her childhood happier. but it had been an error of judgment only which had given him the appearance of harshness, and deprived him of her early love. and now, on really knowing each other, their mutual attachment became very strong. After settling her at Thornton Lacey with every kind attention to her comfort, the object of almost every day was to see her there, or to get her away from it. Selfishly dear as she had long been to Lady Bertram, she could not be parted with willingly by her.
