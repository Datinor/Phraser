But it was not to be avoided: he made her feel that she was the object of all. though she could not say that it was unpleasantly done, that there was indelicacy or ostentation in his manner. and sometimes, when he talked of William, he was really not unagreeable, and shewed even a warmth of heart which did him credit. But still his attentions made no part of her satisfaction. She was happy whenever she looked at William, and saw how perfectly he was enjoying himself, in every five minutes that she could walk about with him and hear his account of his partners.
