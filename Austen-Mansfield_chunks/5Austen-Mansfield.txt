She was preparing for her ninth lyingin. and after bewailing the circumstance, and imploring their countenance as sponsors to the expected child, she could not conceal how important she felt they might be to the future maintenance of the eight already in being. Her eldest was a boy of ten years old, a fine spirited fellow, who longed to be out in the world. but what could she do? Was there any chance of his being hereafter useful to Sir Thomas in the concerns of his West Indian property?
