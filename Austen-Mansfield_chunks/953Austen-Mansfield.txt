The stiffness of the meeting soon gave way before their popular manners and more diffused intimacies: little groups were formed, and everybody grew comfortable. Fanny felt the advantage. and, drawing back from the toils of civility, would have been again most happy, could she have kept her eyes from wandering between Edmund and Mary Crawford. She looked all loveliness--and what might not be the end of it? Her own musings were brought to an end on perceiving Crawford before her, and her thoughts were put into another channel by his engaging her almost instantly for the first two dances.
