Of her two sisters, Price very much more resembled Lady Bertram than Norris. She was a manager by necessity, without any of Mrs. Norris's inclination for it, or any of her activity. Her disposition was naturally easy and indolent, like Lady Bertram's. and a situation of similar affluence and donothingness would have been much more suited to her capacity than the exertions and selfdenials of the one which her imprudent marriage had placed her in.
