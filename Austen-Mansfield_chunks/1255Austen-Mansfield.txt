His avoiding her was quite at an end. He had dined at the Parsonage only the preceding day. After leaving him to his happier thoughts for some minutes, Fanny, feeling it due to herself, returned to Crawford, and said,  It is not merely in temper that I consider him as totally unsuited to myself. though, in that respect, I think the difference between us too great, infinitely too great: his spirits often oppress me.
