The first feeling was disappointment: he had hoped better things. he had thought that an hour's entreaty from a young man like Crawford could not have worked so little change on a gentletempered girl like Fanny. but there was speedy comfort in the determined views and sanguine perseverance of the lover. and when seeing such confidence of success in the principal, Sir Thomas was soon able to depend on it himself. Nothing was omitted, on his side, of civility, compliment, or kindness, that might assist the plan.
