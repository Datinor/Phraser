  Here Fanny interposed, however, with anxious protestations of her own equal ignorance. she had never played the game nor seen it played in her life. and Lady Bertram felt a moment's indecision again. but upon everybody's assuring her that nothing could be so easy, that it was the easiest game on the cards, and Henry Crawford's stepping forward with a most earnest request to be allowed to sit between her ladyship and Miss Price, and teach them both, it was so settled. and Sir Thomas, Mrs. Norris, and Dr. and Grant being seated at the table of prime intellectual state and dignity, the remaining six, under Miss Crawford's direction, were arranged round the other.
