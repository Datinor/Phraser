but Sir Thomas was considering his happiness as very deeply involved in the offence of his sister and friend. cut off by it, as he must be, from the woman whom he had been pursuing with undoubted attachment and strong probability of success. and who, in everything but this despicable brother, would have been so eligible a connexion. He was aware of what Edmund must be suffering on his own behalf, in addition to all the rest, when they were in town: he had seen or conjectured his feelings. and, having reason to think that one interview with Miss Crawford had taken place, from which Edmund derived only increased distress, had been as anxious on that account as on others to get him out of town, and had engaged him in taking Fanny home to her aunt, with a view to his relief and benefit, no less than theirs.
