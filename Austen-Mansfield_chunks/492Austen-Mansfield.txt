and being accompanied by Yates, and followed soon afterwards by Rushworth, Edmund almost immediately took the opportunity of saying,  I cannot, before Yates, speak what I feel as to this play, without reflecting on his friends at Ecclesford. but I must now, my dear Maria, tell you, that I think it exceedingly unfit for private representation, and that I hope you will give it up. I cannot but suppose you will when you have read it carefully over. Read only the first act aloud to either your mother or aunt, and see how you can approve it.
