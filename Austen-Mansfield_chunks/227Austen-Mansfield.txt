but, by walking fifty yards from the hall door, she could look down the park, and command a view of the Parsonage and all its demesnes, gently rising beyond the village road. and in Dr. Grant's meadow she immediately saw the group--Edmund and Miss Crawford both on horseback, riding side by side, Dr. and Grant, and Crawford, with two or three grooms, standing about and looking on. A happy party it appeared to her, all interested in one object: cheerful beyond a doubt, for the sound of merriment ascended even to her. It was a sound which did not make her cheerful. she wondered that Edmund should forget her, and felt a pang.
