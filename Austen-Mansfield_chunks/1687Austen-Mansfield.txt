In this spirit he began the attack, and by animated perseverance had soon reestablished the sort of familiar intercourse, of gallantry, of flirtation, which bounded his views. but in triumphing over the discretion which, though beginning in anger, might have saved them both, he had put himself in the power of feelings on her side more strong than he had supposed. She loved him. there was no withdrawing attentions avowedly dear to her. He was entangled by his own vanity, with as little excuse of love as possible, and without the smallest inconstancy of mind towards her cousin.
