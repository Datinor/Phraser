Julia was to go with them to Brighton. Since rivalry between the sisters had ceased, they had been gradually recovering much of their former good understanding. and were at least sufficiently friends to make each of them exceedingly glad to be with the other at such a time. Some other companion than Rushworth was of the first consequence to his lady. and Julia was quite as eager for novelty and pleasure as Maria, though she might not have struggled through so much to obtain them, and could better bear a subordinate situation.
