CHAPTER XXII  Fanny's consequence increased on the departure of her cousins. Becoming, as she then did, the only young woman in the drawingroom, the only occupier of that interesting division of a family in which she had hitherto held so humble a third, it was impossible for her not to be more looked at, more thought of and attended to, than she had ever been before. and  Another quarter of an hour, and we shall see how it will be. Do not run away the first moment of its holding up.
