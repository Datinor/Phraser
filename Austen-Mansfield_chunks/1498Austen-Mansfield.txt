If Crawford remembered her message to her cousin, she thought it very likely, most likely, that he would write to her at all events. it would be most consistent with his usual kindness. and till she got rid of this idea, till it gradually wore off, by no letters appearing in the course of three or four days more, she was in a most restless, anxious state. At length, a something like composure succeeded. Suspense must be submitted to, and must not be allowed to wear her out, and make her useless.
