She sat some time in a good deal of agitation, listening, trembling, and fearing to be sent for every moment. but as no footsteps approached the East room, she grew gradually composed, could sit down, and be able to employ herself, and able to hope that Crawford had come and would go without her being obliged to know anything of the matter. Nearly half an hour had passed, and she was growing very comfortable, when suddenly the sound of a step in regular approach was heard.
