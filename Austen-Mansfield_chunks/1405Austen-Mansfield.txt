  Fanny had indeed nothing to convey from aunt Norris, but a message to say she hoped that her goddaughter was a good girl, and learnt her book. There had been at one moment a slight murmur in the drawingroom at Mansfield Park about sending her a prayerbook. but no second sound had been heard of such a purpose. Norris, however, had gone home and taken down two old prayerbooks of her husband with that idea. but, upon examination, the ardour of generosity went off.
