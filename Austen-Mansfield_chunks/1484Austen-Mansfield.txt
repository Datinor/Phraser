So anxious for her health and comfort, so very feeling as he now expressed himself, and really seemed, might not it be fairly supposed that he would not much longer persevere in a suit so distressing to her? CHAPTER XLIII  It was presumed that Crawford was travelling back, to London, on the morrow, for nothing more was seen of him at Price's. and two days afterwards, it was a fact ascertained to Fanny by the following letter from his sister, opened and read by her, on another account, with the most anxious curiosity:--   I have to inform you, my dearest Fanny, that Henry has been down to Portsmouth to see you.
