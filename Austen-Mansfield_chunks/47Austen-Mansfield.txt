To her cousins she became occasionally an acceptable companion. Though unworthy, from inferiority of age and strength, to be their constant associate, their pleasures and schemes were sometimes of a nature to make a third very useful, especially when that third was of an obliging, yielding temper. and they could not but own, when their aunt inquired into her faults, or their brother Edmund urged her claims to their kindness, that  Fanny was goodnatured enough.   Edmund was uniformly kind himself.
