I do not know who would ever look at him in the company of the other. I hope I have a better taste than to think of Frank Churchill, who is like nobody by his side. And that you should have been so mistaken, is amazing!--I am sure, but for believing that you entirely approved and meant to encourage me in my attachment, I should have considered it at first too great a presumption almost, to dare to think of him. At first, if you had not told me that more wonderful things had happened. that there had been matches of greater disparity (those were your very words).--I should not have dared to give way to--I should not have thought it possible--But if you, who had been always acquainted with him--
