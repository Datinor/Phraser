And besides the operation of this, as a general principle, you may be sure that Miss Fairfax awes Elton by her superiority both of mind and manner. and that, face to face, Elton treats her with all the respect which she has a claim to. Such a woman as Jane Fairfax probably never fell in Elton's way before--and no degree of vanity can prevent her acknowledging her own comparative littleness in action, if not in consciousness. Yes, any body may know how highly I think of her.
