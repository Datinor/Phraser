May is the very month which Churchill is ordered, or has ordered herself, to spend in some warmer place than Enscombe--in short, to spend in London. so that we have the agreeable prospect of frequent visits from Frank the whole spring--precisely the season of the year which one should have chosen for it: days almost at the longest. weather genial and pleasant, always inviting one out, and never too hot for exercise. When he was here before, we made the best of it. but there was a good deal of wet, damp, cheerless weather.
