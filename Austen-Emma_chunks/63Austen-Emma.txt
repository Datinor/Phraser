These were the ladies whom Emma found herself very frequently able to collect. and happy was she, for her father's sake, in the power. though, as far as she was herself concerned, it was no remedy for the absence of Weston. She was delighted to see her father look comfortable, and very much pleased with herself for contriving things so well. but the quiet prosings of three such women made her feel that every evening so spent was indeed one of the long evenings she had fearfully anticipated.
