Two dances, unfortunately, were all that could be allowed. It was growing late, and Miss Bates became anxious to get home, on her mother's account. After some attempts, therefore, to be permitted to begin again, they were obliged to thank Weston, look sorrowful, and have done.   Perhaps it is as well, I must have asked Miss Fairfax, and her languid dancing would not have agreed with me, after yours.     CHAPTER IX   Emma did not repent her condescension in going to the Coles.
