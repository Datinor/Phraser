Oh! no--I am grieved to find--I was on the point of telling you that when I called at Goddard's door, which I did the very last thing before I returned to dress, I was told that Miss Smith was not better, by no means better, rather worse. Very much grieved and concerned--I had flattered myself that she must be better after such a cordial as I knew had been given her in the morning.   Emma smiled and answered-- My visit was of use to the nervous part of her complaint, I hope.
