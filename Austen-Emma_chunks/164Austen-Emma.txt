Then here is my last, --unclosing a pretty sketch of a gentleman in small size, wholelength-- my last and my best--my brother, John Knightley.--This did not want much of being finished, when I put it away in a pet, and vowed I would never take another likeness. I could not help being provoked. for after all my pains, and when I had really made a very good likeness of it--(Weston and I were quite agreed in thinking it very like)--only too handsome--too flattering--but that was a fault on the right side
