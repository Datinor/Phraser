On their first coming into the country, they had lived in proportion to their income, quietly, keeping little company, and that little unexpensively. but the last year or two had brought them a considerable increase of means--the house in town had yielded greater profits, and fortune in general had smiled on them. With their wealth, their views increased. their want of a larger house, their inclination for more company. They added to their house, to their number of servants, to their expenses of every sort.
