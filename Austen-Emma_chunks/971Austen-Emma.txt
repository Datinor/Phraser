 And away he ran. Long before he reappeared, attending the short, neat, briskmoving aunt, and her elegant niece,--Weston, like a sweettempered woman and a good wife, had examined the passage again, and found the evils of it much less than she had supposed before--indeed very trifling. and here ended the difficulties of decision. All the rest, in speculation at least, was perfectly smooth. All the minor arrangements of table and chair, lights and music, tea and supper, made themselves.
