It was by no means her object to have it believed that her sister was not a fine lady. perhaps there was want of spirit in the pretence of it.--and she was considering in what way she had best retract, when Mr. Weston went on.   Churchill is not much in my good graces, as you may suspect--but this is quite between ourselves. She is very fond of Frank, and therefore I would not speak ill of her. Besides, she is out of health now.
