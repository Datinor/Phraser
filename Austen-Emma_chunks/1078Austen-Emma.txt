Her father was growing nervous, and could not understand her. Her mind returned to Elton's offences, and long, very long, did they occupy her. CHAPTER XV   Emma was not required, by any subsequent discovery, to retract her ill opinion of Elton. Her observation had been pretty correct. Such as Elton appeared to her on this second interview, such she appeared whenever they met again,--selfimportant, presuming, familiar, ignorant, and illbred.
