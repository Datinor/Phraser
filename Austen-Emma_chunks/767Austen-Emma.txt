CHAPTER VIII   Frank Churchill came back again. and if he kept his father's dinner waiting, it was not known at Hartfield. for Weston was too anxious for his being a favourite with Woodhouse, to betray any imperfection which could be concealed. He came back, had had his hair cut, and laughed at himself with a very good grace, but without seeming really at all ashamed of what he had done. He had no reason to wish his hair longer, to conceal any confusion of face.
