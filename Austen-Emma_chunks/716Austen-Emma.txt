And how did you think Miss Fairfax looking? Ill, very ill--that is, if a young lady can ever be allowed to look ill. But the expression is hardly admissible, Weston, is it? Ladies can never look ill. And, seriously, Miss Fairfax is naturally so pale, as almost always to give the appearance of ill health.--A most deplorable want of complexion.   Emma would not agree to this, and began a warm defence of Miss Fairfax's complexion.
