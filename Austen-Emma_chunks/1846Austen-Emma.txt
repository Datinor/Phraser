The others had been talking of the child, Weston giving an account of a little alarm she had been under, the evening before, from the infant's appearing not quite well. She believed she had been foolish, but it had alarmed her, and she had been within half a minute of sending for Perry. Perhaps she ought to be ashamed, but Weston had been almost as uneasy as herself.--In ten minutes, however, the child had been perfectly well again.
