I think you must know Hartfield enough to comprehend that. A straightforward, openhearted man like Weston, and a rational, unaffected woman like Miss Taylor, may be safely left to manage their own concerns. You are more likely to have done harm to yourself, than good to them, by interference. Emma never thinks of herself, if she can do good to others, But, my dear, pray do not make any more matches. they are silly things, and break up one's family circle grievously.
