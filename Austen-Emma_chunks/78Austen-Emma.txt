Harriet had no penetration. She had been satisfied to hear and believe just what Goddard chose to tell her. and looked no farther. Goddard, and the teachers, and the girls and the affairs of the school in general, formed naturally a great part of the conversation--and but for her acquaintance with the Martins of AbbeyMill Farm, it must have been the whole. But the Martins occupied her thoughts a good deal.
