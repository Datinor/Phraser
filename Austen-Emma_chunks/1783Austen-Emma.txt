on her first arrival she had thought her out of spirits, which appeared perfectly natural, as there was a dentist to be consulted. but, since that business had been over, she did not appear to find Harriet different from what she had known her before.--Isabella, to be sure, was no very quick observer. yet if Harriet had not been equal to playing with the children, it would not have escaped her. Emma's comforts and hopes were most agreeably carried on, by Harriet's being to stay longer. her fortnight was likely to be a month at least.
