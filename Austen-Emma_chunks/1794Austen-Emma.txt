but the worst was overcome, the idea was given. time and continual repetition must do the rest.--To Emma's entreaties and assurances succeeded Knightley's, whose fond praise of her gave the subject even a kind of welcome. and he was soon used to be talked to by each, on every fair occasion.--They had all the assistance which Isabella could give, by letters of the strongest approbation. and Weston was ready, on the first meeting, to consider the subject in the most serviceable light--first, as a settled, and, secondly, as a good one--well aware of the nearly equal importance of the two recommendations to Woodhouse's mind.--It was agreed upon, as what was to be. and every body by whom he was used to be guided assuring him that it would be for his happiness.
