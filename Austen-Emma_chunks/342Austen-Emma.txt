  Harriet, she found, had never in her life been inside the Vicarage, and her curiosity to see it was so extreme, that, considering exteriors and probabilities, Emma could only class it, as a proof of love, with Elton's seeing ready wit in her.   I wish we could contrive it, but I cannot think of any tolerable pretence for going in.--no servant that I want to inquire about of his housekeeper--no message from my father.   She pondered, but could think of nothing. After a mutual silence of some minutes, Harriet thus began again--   I do so wonder, Miss Woodhouse, that you should not be married, or going to be married!
