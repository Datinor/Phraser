She was not a woman of strong understanding or any quickness. and with this resemblance of her father, she inherited also much of his constitution. was delicate in her own health, overcareful of that of her children, had many fears and many nerves, and was as fond of her own Wingfield in town as her father could be of Perry. They were alike too, in a general benevolence of temper, and a strong habit of regard for every old acquaintance. John Knightley was a tall, gentlemanlike, and very clever man.
