When she took in her history, indeed, her situation, as well as her beauty. when she considered what all this elegance was destined to, what she was going to sink from, how she was going to live, it seemed impossible to feel any thing but compassion and respect. especially, if to every wellknown particular entitling her to interest, were added the highly probable circumstance of an attachment to Dixon, which she had so naturally started to herself. In that case, nothing could be more pitiable or more honourable than the sacrifices she had resolved on.
