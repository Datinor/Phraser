There was no want of likeness, she had been fortunate in the attitude, and as she meant to throw in a little improvement to the figure, to give a little more height, and considerably more elegance, she had great confidence of its being in every way a pretty drawing at last, and of its filling its destined place with credit to them both--a standing memorial of the beauty of one, the skill of the other, and the friendship of both. with as many other agreeable associations as Elton's very promising attachment was likely to add. Harriet was to sit again the next day. and Elton, just as he ought, entreated for the permission of attending and reading to them again.   By all means.
