They might be more safely held up as a pattern. There is an openness, a quickness, almost a bluntness in Weston, which every body likes in him, because there is so much goodhumour with it--but that would not do to be copied. Neither would Knightley's downright, decided, commanding sort of manner, though it suits him very well. his figure, and look, and situation in life seem to allow it. but if any young man were to set about copying him, he would not be sufferable.
