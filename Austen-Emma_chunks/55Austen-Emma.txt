He had been at the pains of consulting Perry, the apothecary, on the subject. Perry was an intelligent, gentlemanlike man, whose frequent visits were one of the comforts of Woodhouse's life. and upon being applied to, he could not but acknowledge (though it seemed rather against the bias of inclination) that weddingcake might certainly disagree with many--perhaps with most people, unless taken moderately. With such an opinion, in confirmation of his own, Woodhouse hoped to influence every visitor of the newly married pair.
