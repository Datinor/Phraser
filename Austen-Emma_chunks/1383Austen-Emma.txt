  Knightley had another reason for avoiding a table in the shade. He wished to persuade Woodhouse, as well as Emma, to join the party. and he knew that to have any of them sitting down out of doors to eat would inevitably make him ill. Woodhouse must not, under the specious pretence of a morning drive, and an hour or two spent at Donwell, be tempted away to his misery. He was invited on good faith. No lurking horrors were to upbraid him for his easy credulity.
