nor did it occupy Emma long to convince him that they might in one of the carriages find room for Harriet also. Harriet, Elton, and Knightley, their own especial set, were the only persons invited to meet them.--the hours were to be early, as well as the numbers few. Woodhouse's habits and inclination being consulted in every thing. The evening before this great event (for it was a very great event that Woodhouse should dine out, on the 24th of December) had been spent by Harriet at Hartfield, and she had gone home so much indisposed with a cold, that, but for her own earnest wish of being nursed by Mrs. Goddard, Emma could not have allowed her to leave the house. Emma called on her the next day, and found her doom already signed with regard to Randalls.
