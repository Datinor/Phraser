CHAPTER IX   Emma's pensive meditations, as she walked home, were not interrupted. but on entering the parlour, she found those who must rouse her. Mr. Knightley and Harriet had arrived during her absence, and were sitting with her father.--Knightley immediately got up, and in a manner decidedly graver than usual, said,   I would not go away without seeing you, but I have no time to spare, and therefore must now be gone directly.
