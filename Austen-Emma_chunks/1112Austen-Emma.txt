Well, Weston, what do you say now to Knightley's marrying Jane Fairfax? Why, really, dear Emma, I say that he is so very much occupied by the idea of not being in love with her, that I should not wonder if it were to end in his being so at last. Do not beat me.     CHAPTER XVI   Every body in and about Highbury who had ever visited Elton, was disposed to pay him attention on his marriage. Dinnerparties and eveningparties were made for him and his lady.
