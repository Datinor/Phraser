Well, that is so very!--I am sure if Jane is tired, you will be so kind as to give her your arm.--Elton, and Miss Hawkins!--Good morning to you.   Emma, alone with her father, had half her attention wanted by him while he lamented that young people would be in such a hurry to marry--and to marry strangers too--and the other half she could give to her own view of the subject. It was to herself an amusing and a very welcome piece of news, as proving that Elton could not have suffered long. but she was sorry for Harriet: Harriet must feel it--and all that she could hope was, by giving the first information herself, to save her from hearing it abruptly from others. It was now about the time that she was likely to call.
