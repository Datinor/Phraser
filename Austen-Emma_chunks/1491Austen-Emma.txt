'If you must go, my dear,' said I, 'you must, and I will say you are laid down upon the bed.'   Emma was most sincerely interested. Her heart had been long growing kinder towards Jane. and this picture of her present sufferings acted as a cure of every former ungenerous suspicion, and left her nothing but pity. and the remembrance of the less just and less gentle sensations of the past, obliged her to admit that Jane might very naturally resolve on seeing Cole or any other steady friend, when she might not bear to see herself.
