How is Woodhouse?--I am so glad to hear such a good account. Weston told me you were here.--Oh! then, said I, I must run across, I am sure Miss Woodhouse will allow me just to run across and entreat her to come in. my mother will be so very happy to see her--and now we are such a nice party, she cannot refuse.--'Aye, pray do,' said Frank Churchill, 'Miss Woodhouse's opinion of the instrument will be worth having.' --But, said I, I shall be more sure of succeeding if one of you will go with me.--'Oh,' said he, 'wait half a minute, till I have finished my job.'
