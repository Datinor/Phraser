Dixon.--Very well. Yes, I immediately perceive that it must be the joint present of and Dixon. We were speaking the other day, you know, of his being so warm an admirer of her performance. Yes, and what you told me on that head, confirmed an idea which I had entertained before.--I do not mean to reflect upon the good intentions of either Dixon or Miss Fairfax, but I cannot help suspecting either that, after making his proposals to her friend, he had the misfortune to fall in love with her, or that he became conscious of a little attachment on her side. One might guess twenty things without guessing exactly the right.
