but she left her with every previous resolution confirmed of being humble and discreet, and repressing imagination all the rest of her life. Her second duty now, inferior only to her father's claims, was to promote Harriet's comfort, and endeavour to prove her own affection in some better method than by matchmaking. She got her to Hartfield, and shewed her the most unvarying kindness, striving to occupy and amuse her, and by books and conversation, to drive Elton from her thoughts. Time, she knew, must be allowed for this being thoroughly done. and she could suppose herself but an indifferent judge of such matters in general, and very inadequate to sympathise in an attachment to Elton in particular.
