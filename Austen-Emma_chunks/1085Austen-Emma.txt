We must not allow them to be verified in sweet Jane Fairfax. I cannot think there is any danger of it, and when you are better acquainted with Miss Fairfax's situation and understand what her home has been, with Colonel and Campbell, I have no idea that you will suppose her talents can be unknown. Oh! but dear Miss Woodhouse, she is now in such retirement, such obscurity, so thrown away.--Whatever advantages she may have enjoyed with the Campbells are so palpably at an end! And I think she feels it.
