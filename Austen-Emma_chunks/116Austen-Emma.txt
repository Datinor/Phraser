He had a comfortable home for her, and Emma imagined a very sufficient income. for though the vicarage of Highbury was not large, he was known to have some independent property. and she thought very highly of him as a goodhumoured, wellmeaning, respectable young man, without any deficiency of useful understanding or knowledge of the world. She had already satisfied herself that he thought Harriet a beautiful girl, which she trusted, with such frequent meetings at Hartfield, was foundation enough on his side.
