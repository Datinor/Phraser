and the quarter of an hour quite convinced her that Elton was a vain woman, extremely well satisfied with herself, and thinking much of her own importance. that she meant to shine and be very superior, but with manners which had been formed in a bad school, pert and familiar. that all her notions were drawn from one set of people, and one style of living. that if not foolish she was ignorant, and that her society would certainly do Elton no good. Harriet would have been a better match.
