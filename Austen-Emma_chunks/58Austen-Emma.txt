Real, longstanding regard brought the Westons and Knightley. and by Elton, a young man living alone without liking it, the privilege of exchanging any vacant evening of his own blank solitude for the elegancies and society of Woodhouse's drawingroom, and the smiles of his lovely daughter, was in no danger of being thrown away. After these came a second set. among the most comeatable of whom were and Miss Bates, and Goddard, three ladies almost always at the service of an invitation from Hartfield, and who were fetched and carried home so often, that Woodhouse thought it no hardship for either James or the horses.
