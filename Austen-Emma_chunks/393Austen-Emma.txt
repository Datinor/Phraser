CHAPTER XII   Knightley was to dine with them--rather against the inclination of Woodhouse, who did not like that any one should share with him in Isabella's first day. Emma's sense of right however had decided it. and besides the consideration of what was due to each brother, she had particular pleasure, from the circumstance of the late disagreement between Knightley and herself, in procuring him the proper invitation. She hoped they might now become friends again.
