Mr. Knightley saw no such passion, and of course thought nothing of its effects. but she saw too much of it to feel a doubt of its overcoming any hesitations that a reasonable prudence might originally suggest. and more than a reasonable, becoming degree of prudence, she was very sure did not belong to Elton. Harriet's cheerful look and manner established hers: she came back, not to think of Martin, but to talk of Elton.
