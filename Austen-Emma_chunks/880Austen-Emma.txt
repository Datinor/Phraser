said he directly, 'there is nothing in the way of fruit half so good, and these are the finestlooking homebaked apples I ever saw in my life.' That, you know, was so very.... And I am sure, by his manner, it was no compliment. Indeed they are very delightful apples, and Wallis does them full justice--only we do not have them baked more than twice, and Woodhouse made us promise to have them done three times--but Miss Woodhouse will be so good as not to mention it. The apples themselves are the very finest sort for baking, beyond a doubt. all from Donwell--some of Mr. Knightley's most liberal supply.
