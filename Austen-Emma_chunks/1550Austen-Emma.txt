  Weston looked up, afraid to believe. but Emma's countenance was as steady as her words.   That you may have less difficulty in believing this boast, of my present perfect indifference, I will farther tell you, that there was a period in the early part of our acquaintance, when I did like him, when I was very much disposed to be attached to him--nay, was attached--and how it came to cease, is perhaps the wonder. Fortunately, however, it did cease. I have really for some time past, for at least these three months, cared nothing about him.
