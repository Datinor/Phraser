Jane Fairfax had already quitted Highbury, and was restored to the comforts of her beloved home with the Campbells.--The Churchills were also in town. and they were only waiting for November. The intermediate month was the one fixed on, as far as they dared, by Emma and Knightley.--They had determined that their marriage ought to be concluded while John and Isabella were still at Hartfield, to allow them the fortnight's absence in a tour to the seaside, which was the plan.--John and Isabella, and every other friend, were agreed in approving it. But Woodhouse--how was Woodhouse to be induced to consent?--he, who had never yet alluded to their marriage but as a distant event. When first sounded on the subject, he was so miserable, that they were almost hopeless.--A second allusion, indeed, gave less pain.--He began to think it was to be, and that he could not prevent it--a very promising step of the mind on its way to resignation.
