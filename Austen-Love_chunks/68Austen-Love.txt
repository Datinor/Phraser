As soon as we had packed up our wardrobe and valuables, we left Macdonald Hall, and after having walked about a mile and a half we sate down by the side of a clear limpid stream to refresh our exhausted limbs. The place was suited to meditation. A grove of fullgrown Elms sheltered us from the East--. A Bed of fullgrown Nettles from the West--. Before us ran the murmuring brook and behind us ran the turnpike road.
